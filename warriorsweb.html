Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) was the founder of the Maratha Empire in western India. Known for his progressive administration, brilliant military tactics, and naval strategies, Shivaji Maharaj carved out a kingdom that challenged the mighty Mughal Empire.

  • Born: February 19, 1630
  • Founded the Maratha Empire in 1674
  • Built strong forts like Raigad, Pratapgad, and Sinhagad
  • Introduced a disciplined and people-friendly administration

Maharana Pratap

Maharana Pratap of Mewar is remembered for his valor and resistance against the Mughal Emperor Akbar. The Battle of Haldighati (1576) remains one of the most significant chapters of Rajput bravery and sacrifice.

  • Born: May 9, 1540
  • Ruled Mewar: 1572 – 1597
  • Fought the Battle of Haldighati in 1576
  • Known for his horse "Chetak"

Krishnadevaraya

Krishnadevaraya (reigned 1509–1529) was the greatest ruler of the Vijayanagara Empire. He expanded the empire through successful military campaigns and brought it to its peak of power, culture, and prosperity. Known as an able administrator and patron of art and literature, he encouraged scholars in multiple languages including Telugu, Sanskrit, Kannada, and Tamil.

  • Born: 1471
  • Ruled: 1509 – 1529
  • Expanded Vijayanagara to its greatest territorial extent
  • Authored the Telugu epic "Amuktamalyada"

Quick Facts

  • Shivaji Maharaj emphasized "Swarajya" (self-rule).
  • Maharana Pratap’s horse "Chetak" is legendary.
  • Krishnadevaraya was called "Andhra Bhoja" for his patronage of literature.

Did You Know?

Krishnadevaraya’s reign is often called the "Golden Age of Telugu Literature."
